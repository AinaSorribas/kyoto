Gion

Gion is a historic district located in Kyoto, Japan, known for its traditional wooden machiya merchant houses and geisha culture. The district is lined with charming streets and alleys, which are perfect for leisurely strolls. Visitors can admire the beautiful architecture and get a feel for what Kyoto was like in the past.

Gion is also famous for its geisha culture. Visitors can catch a glimpse of geiko (geisha) or maiko (apprentice geisha) on their way to a performance or appointment. There are also several teahouses and restaurants in the area where visitors can experience traditional Japanese culture and cuisine, and even book a private dinner with a geisha.

In addition to the traditional atmosphere, Gion also offers modern attractions such as art galleries, boutiques, and cafes. Visitors can explore the contemporary side of Gion while still appreciating the traditional charm of the district. Gion is a must-visit destination for anyone who wants to experience the beauty and culture of Kyoto.

What to do?

  • Experience the traditional geisha culture: Gion is known for its geisha culture, and visitors can experience it firsthand by visiting a teahouse or booking a private dinner with a geisha. Watching a geisha performance or conversing with one over a cup of tea can be a memorable and enriching experience.
  • Stroll along the charming streets and alleys: Gion is a picturesque district filled with narrow streets and traditional wooden machiya merchant houses. Take a leisurely stroll through the streets and admire the beautiful architecture, including the iconic lattice windows and sliding doors.
  • Explore the modern side of Gion: While Gion is steeped in history and tradition, it also offers modern attractions such as art galleries, boutiques, and cafes. Visit contemporary galleries showcasing local artists, browse unique and fashionable boutiques, and enjoy a cup of coffee at a trendy cafe. The blend of old and new creates a unique atmosphere in Gion that is not to be missed.

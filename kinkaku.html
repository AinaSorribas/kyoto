Kinkaku-ji

Kinkaku-ji, also known as the Temple of the Golden Pavilion, is a Zen Buddhist temple located in Kyoto, Japan. The temple is famous for its stunning architecture, with the top two floors covered entirely in gold leaf, which shimmers in the sunlight and reflects off the surrounding pond. The temple was originally built as a retirement villa for a shogun in the 14th century and was later converted into a temple after his death.

Visitors to Kinkaku-ji can take a leisurely stroll through the beautiful gardens surrounding the temple, which were designed to complement the architecture and create a peaceful atmosphere. The gardens feature a variety of trees, plants, and flowers, including cherry blossoms in the spring and vibrant red and orange leaves in the autumn. There are also several smaller temples and shrines on the grounds, as well as a teahouse where visitors can enjoy a traditional Japanese tea ceremony.

Kinkaku-ji is one of the most popular tourist attractions in Kyoto and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The temple and its surroundings are particularly beautiful during the autumn foliage season, when the leaves on the trees turn bright red, orange, and yellow. Visitors can also see the temple illuminated at night during special events throughout the year, adding an extra touch of magic to the already enchanting atmosphere.

What to do?

  • Take a hike through the torii gates: Fushimi Inari is famous for its thousands of vermilion torii gates that create a pathway through the forest and up the mountain. Take a leisurely hike along the path and enjoy the stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Make an offering and pray for good fortune: Fushimi Inari is a Shinto shrine dedicated to the god of agriculture, business, and prosperity. Visitors can make an offering, such as a small donation or a wooden plaque with a wish written on it, and offer a prayer for good fortune and success.
  • Try the local food: Fushimi Inari is surrounded by a lively atmosphere with bustling food stalls selling a variety of delicious treats. Sample local delicacies such as grilled mochi, takoyaki (octopus balls), and matcha soft serve ice cream. You can also find souvenir shops selling various items like amulets and good luck charms to take home with you.
